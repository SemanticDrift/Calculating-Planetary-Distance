## What This Does

Introduces a frequency-phase method for calculating planetary distances using harmonic resonance and wave interference. Treating the solar system as a standing wave where distance is the spatial manifestation of inverse frequency, orbital distances for all major bodies are derived with greater than 99.7% precision. No gravitational constants, mass terms, or force equations are required.

---

## The Model

The solar system is modeled as a dual-chamber resonant oscillator separated by a phase inversion boundary at approximately 2.8 AU.

**Inner Chamber (Mercury to Mars):** Distances follow harmonic ratios governed by the 1/6 and phi duality, where 1/6 represents radial closure and phi represents transcendental expansion.

**Outer Chamber (Jupiter to Pluto):** Distances follow the direct inverse frequency law: r = 1/f

**Torque Displacement Node:** The Asteroid Belt is identified as a structural necessity, not a failed planet. Phase inversion at 2.8 AU prevents planetary coalescence. Ceres at 2.77 AU marks the node center.
